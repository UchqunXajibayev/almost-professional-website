About Me

I’m a student who grew up obsessed with taking things apart — electronics, devices, tools, anything mechanical. I learn by doing, breaking, fixing, and rebuilding.

My main interests focus on engineering, robotics, system security, and hands-on technical problem-solving. I don’t like learning things only from books — I prefer building and experimenting until I actually understand how something works.

Outside competitions and school, I spend most of my time improving my technical skills: working with Linux, exploring cybersecurity, testing network behavior, and building projects that challenge me to think deeper.

I’m focused on growth, discipline, and becoming someone who can contribute to real engineering work — not just pass exams. I like solving problems where there’s no clear answer and learning tools that real engineers use every day.

My long-term goal is to study engineering at MIT — not for the name, but because I want to build, discover, and push myself beyond the limits of a normal school environment.
