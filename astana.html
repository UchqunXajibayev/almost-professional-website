Spike Robot Competition — Astana

The Astana competition was my first major international robotics experience and the moment I realized how much I wanted engineering to be part of my life. Representing Uzbekistan wasn’t just an honor — it forced me to grow fast and take real responsibility for my work.

Our team built a Spike Prime robot from scratch. I focused on mechanical design, gear balancing, sensor placement, and precision turning. Every day was hands-on: testing, adjusting, breaking, rebuilding, rewriting code, and improving movement accuracy. Nothing worked perfectly the first time, which taught me how real engineering is done.

The competition was intense — timed rounds, quick strategy changes, new challenges thrown at us constantly. I had to think clearly under pressure, solve problems quickly, and communicate with my team in a calm and focused way.

Astana didn’t just give me experience. It built my confidence. It showed me that I enjoy solving tough problems, leading technical decisions, and competing in an environment where skill actually matters.
