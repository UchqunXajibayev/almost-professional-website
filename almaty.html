Spike Robot Competition — Almaty

Almaty was on a completely different level from Astana. The teams were more experienced, the challenges were harder, and every point mattered. This competition pushed me far beyond what I thought my limits were.

I upgraded our robot dramatically — optimized turning accuracy, improved attachment stability, rewrote movement sequences, and experimented with proportional control. I spent hours testing every angle, making sure our movements were consistent down to the smallest detail.

Our team ended up placing second among strong international competitors. It wasn’t luck — it was hours of debugging, rebuilding, testing, and learning from failures. The moment we received the certificate that qualified us toward the Australia finals felt unreal. That second-place finish means more to me than any medal I’ve ever won.

Almaty taught me how to compete like an engineer: stay calm, adapt instantly, and think logically even when things go wrong. It made me want to push my skills even further.
